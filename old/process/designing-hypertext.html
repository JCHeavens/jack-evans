Designing Hypertext

25 June 2015

On the first website, Tim Berners-Lee defines the web as “a wide-area hypermedia information retrieval initiative aiming to give universal access to a large universe of documents.”

For effective websites, then, we must design according to this central mechanism Berners-Lee defines.

Hypermedia—hypertext, specifically, since the web was created to be a typographic medium—is the essence of the web. So, when I make websites, the body text link is the first component I design.

Antiquated? Maybe. But in this nightmarish age of sponsored listicles masquerading as “content”, doesn’t a well-set, underlined, electric blue link that takes you where you want to go sound nice?
